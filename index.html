Which artist does Morrissey cite as a key influence
on his coming of age?
  • Ian Curtis
  • John Lennon
  • David Bowie
  • Aretha Franklin

After seeing Bowie as a guest on Ayshea Brough's color TV show, Morrissey became aware of a world of culture, art, and identity outside of the one in which he grew up. He writes,


"As David Bowie appears, the child dies. The vision is profound- a sanity heralding the coming of consciousness of someone who- at last!- transcends our gloomy coal-fire existence. David Bowie is detached from everything, yet open to everything; stripped of the notion that both art and life are impossible. He is quite real, impossibly glamorous, fearless, and quite British. How could this possibly be?" p62
When Morrissey sang with an unnamed band at his first public gig, at Manchester University in 1981, what name did the newspapers give the group?
  • The Nosebleeds
  • The Heartbreakers
  • Manchester Boys
  • Grey Horse

Paul Morley, of the New Musical Express reviewed the event and dubbed the band "The Nosebleeds", which was actually the name of the group's rhythm section's former band. In Morrissey's own words,


"The band is listed as the Nosebleeds, and I am lumbered with this miscued name in private sufferance forevermore." p62
What's the origin of the band name "The Smiths"?
  • It was the guitarist's last name
  • It pays homage to the English linguist Albert Hugh Smith
  • It was a common name with little cultural meaning
  • It references Morrissey's uncle and father, who were blacksmiths in Ireland.

No band member could think of anything better, and Morrissey suggested the Smiths because of its general lack of meaning or connection as a common surname. He writes,


"It strikes me that the Smiths name lacks any settled association on face value, yet could also suit a presentation of virtually any style of music. It sounded like a timeless name, unlikely to date, and unlikely to glue itself to come-and-go movements." p147
Which album was the Smiths' first to reach the top of the UK charts?

Meat Is Murder was the first and only #1 album in the UK during the band's lifetime. Though it never broke the top 100 in the US, it is included in Rolling Stone's list of 500 Best Albums of All Time.
